Into The Babyverse

Space Shooter
Unreal C++ Blueprint

Overview

Into The Babyverse is a game taking place inside a baby's ingenious yet innocent imagination. You play as a baby space explorer who is on a mission to rescue his planet-shaped toys from being abducted by the aliens. This game is a inspired version of a popular game for toddlers called Rock-a-Stack. Through this game, I wanted to achieve a fast-paced shooter game with some absurd elements derived from the baby's life
Role: Solo Developer, Capstone
Skills: Unreal, 6DOF Movement, Spline Management, 3D Physics, Optimization
Drive Link: Into The Babyverse

Onboarding

The onboarding process starts with a tutorial for movement, shooting, boosting, and hooking mechanics using keyboard and mouse input. In the tutorial, the player has to press the key doing a specific action and as they keep on pressing it, the tutorial progresses forward. Along with an introduction, each mechanic is thoroughly practiced to make the player understand and master it properly.

Cutscene

Cutscene was added to the game to make the whole alien invasion concept impactful. It starts with a camera transition from player to the approaching alien spaceship which starts shooting bullets at the Sun and an explosion begins. It is done by having a yellow sphere expand rapidly at the center of the Sun and applying a shader with depth pass disabled to make it visible all the time. To give a sense of exploding energy, exposure on the camera is increased inversely to its distance from the explosion object's circumference.

Game Loop

The game loop includes saving the planets from getting sucked inside the blackhole by guiding them inside the toy bucket by either shooting bullets at them or using a hook and pull. To help the players, there is a UI displaying the nearest planet to the Blackhole and its distance from it. The caveat is that the toy bucket is designed in a way that if the proper order is not followed, all the planets will not fit inside it.
